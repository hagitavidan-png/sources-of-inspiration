✦ The Curriculum

Seven Units. One Big Question:
Where Does Creativity Come From?

Each unit explores a different source of artistic inspiration — guiding students from outer observation to deep inner expression.

1
❤️
Unit 01
Emotion as Inspiration

How do artists use feelings as raw material? Students explore how emotion shapes color, line, and composition — from Munch's Scream to their own visual diaries.

6 lessons 45–60 min each
What colour is sadness?
The art of Edvard Munch
Creating an emotional landscape

Unit 02
Nature as Inspiration

From Monet's water lilies to the geometry of a leaf. Students discover how the natural world has inspired artists across all movements and periods.

6 lessons 45–60 min each
Patterns in nature & art
Monet and the Impressionists
Botanical drawing & abstraction

🎵
Unit 03
Music & Rhythm in Art

Can you paint a sound? Students bridge the senses — exploring Kandinsky's synesthesia, jazz-inspired abstract art, and how rhythm becomes visual movement.

6 lessons 45–60 min each
Kandinsky & synesthesia
Painting music: jazz & abstract
Rhythm, repetition, movement
4
🧠
Unit 04
Memory & Identity

Art as autobiography. Students explore how Frida Kahlo, Marc Chagall and others transformed memory into visual storytelling — and create their own identity portraits.

6 lessons 45–60 min each
Frida Kahlo – self as subject
Memory map exercise
Creating an identity portrait

🌍
Unit 05
Culture & Symbols

Art as cultural language. Students decode visual symbols from world traditions and create work that honors and reimagines cultural heritage.

6 lessons 45–60 min each
Visual symbols across cultures
Folk art & modern remix
My cultural symbol project

Personal Style Development

The final unit. Students synthesize everything they've learned to develop their own artistic voice and create a capstone portfolio piece.

6 lessons 45–60 min each
Finding your visual language
Artist statement writing
Final portfolio project
