Unit: Personal Experience as Inspiration

Memory is made
of parts.

You can draw them.

Matisse

Memory is never pure.

— Henri Matisse

That is exactly what makes it interesting.

👁

Look at these drawings — What did the artist choose to show?

Parts overlapping
Not everything is real
Memory without order

Not everything is clear. What stays with you?

Think of yourself

Choose a memory.

You don't need to remember everything.

Break it apart

What do you
remember?

Choose a few. Not all.

Start

Draw the parts
you chose.

No need for order.

  • Draw each part separately — any size.
  • Don't connect them yet.
  • Draw fast. Don't think too long.

Arrange them

How do the
parts connect?

Next to each other
On top of each other
Far and close
Mixed / no rule

Keep going

Add and change.

Memory does not have to be accurate.

  • Change the size of a part — make it bigger or smaller.
  • Add something you imagined, not only what happened.
  • Erase or cover something if it doesn't feel right.

Make it yours

Add something small
that only you understand.

No explanation needed.

Pause

Stop and look
at your drawing.

Just look. Don't judge.

What you made

This drawing does not show
what happened.

It shows how you remember.
That is your source of inspiration.

Reflection

Which part is strongest
in your drawing?


Saved.

Lesson complete

Your memory is
now a drawing.

You remembered in parts.
You made something no one else could make.
