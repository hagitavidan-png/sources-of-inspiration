Lesson 2.4

Some things are
larger than you.

Sea. Mountain. Sky.

Step 1

Think of a place that made
you feel small.

Sea, mountain, sky.
A vast open space.

Reference

Caspar David Friedrich

Wanderer above the Sea of Fog, 1818

The figure stands before a vast landscape. We don't see the face — only the encounter.

Reference

J. M. W. Turner

Snow Storm: Steam-Boat off a Harbour's Mouth, 1842

Here, the figure almost disappears. Light, wind, and motion take over.

Step 2

Draw a large space.

Add something small inside it —
it could be you.

Step 3

Where is the
small element placed?

Pause

What dominates
the drawing?

The space dominates
The figure is felt
A balance between them

Insight

Placement
changes the feeling.

A small figure in a vast space —

creates a feeling of the sublime.

Where you place it changes everything.

Sometimes being small
is what allows you
to see something vast.

That's the sublime.
