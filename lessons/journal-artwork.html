Unit: Personal Experience as Inspiration

A personal journal
builds over time.

Moments from it can become an artwork.

On drawing

I draw what I live.

— Hundertwasser

Everything you live through is material.

📖

Look at the pages — Different moments together on one page.

Drawings and marks together
Open and unfinished
Words, shapes, color

What do you notice on each page?

Think about your week

Which moments
are you taking from it?

Choose one or two.

Collect the pieces

Choose a few things
from your week.

Not all. Just a few.

Begin

Place them
on the page.

Draw, write, or mark.
No need for order.

  • Each piece can be any size.
  • You can draw it, write it, or just mark it.
  • Let the page fill slowly.

How do they sit

How do the parts
sit on the page?

Near each other
On top of each other
Far apart
Mixed / no rule

Keep adding

Keep adding
and changing.

The page can stay open and unfinished.

  • Add a new piece next to something already there.
  • You can change the size of something.
  • Leave empty space — it belongs there too.

Make it yours

Add something small
that only you understand.

No need to explain it.

Pause

Stop and look
at your page.

Just look. Don't judge.

What you made

This is not one image —
it is part of your time.

This is your source of inspiration.

Reflection

What stands out most
on your page?


Saved.

Lesson complete

Your journal page
is now an artwork.

You took moments from your week.
You made something only you could make.
