Unit: Personal Experience as Inspiration

A moment becomes
a drawing.

It starts with a single line.

Van Gogh

I want to paint what I feel,
not what I see.

— Vincent van Gogh

What would YOUR feeling look like as a line?

👁

Look at these lines — How do they feel?

Heavy, agitated lines
Sharp, explosive lines
Soft, flowing lines

Calm? Tense? Moving?

Think of yourself

Choose a moment.

A small thing. Real. Yours.

Name it

What did you feel?

Choose your line

What kind of line
is your feeling?

Soft / Curved
Sharp / Broken
Straight / Strong
Many / Scattered
Fast / Repeated
Slow / Continuous

Now draw

Draw the feeling.
Not the story.

Use the lines you chose. Fill the page.

  • Draw only with lines — no shapes or figures.
  • Let the line move fast or slow — feel it.
  • Don't think. Just draw.

Keep going

Mix and change.

Add new lines. Cover some. Change direction.

  • Try combining two types of lines.
  • Let some lines be quiet and some be loud.
  • The drawing doesn't need to look like anything.

Look at color — How does the color move?

Bold / Covering
Transparent / Spreading
Splashed / Blended

Quiet? Exploding? Bleeding into the paper?

Add a layer

Now — add color.

On top of your lines.

  • Transparent wash: let the lines show through.
  • Opaque fill: cover parts you want to hide.
  • Blending: let two colors merge at the edges.
  • The color doesn't have to be "correct".

Keep working

Keep going.
Don't stop yet.

Let the color and lines build together.

  • You can go back and add more lines now.
  • Let some areas stay empty.
  • This is a conversation between color and line.

Make it yours

Add one small
personal mark.

Something only you understand.
No explanation needed.

Pause

Stop.
Look at your drawing.

Don't judge. Just look.

Connect

Where is the moment
in your drawing?

Not the story — the feeling.
Can you see it in the lines? The color?

Reflection

What did you discover?

Write one sentence. Or two.


Saved.

Lesson complete

Your emotion
is now a drawing.

You started with a feeling.
You ended with something no one else could make.
